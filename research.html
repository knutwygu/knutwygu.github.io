Agentic Affordance: Why LLMs Invite the Intentional Stance (with Aileen C. Luo)

Philosophy of AI Affordance Theory Human-AI Interaction

// Abstract

This paper develops the concept of "agentic affordance" to explain why humans naturally attribute intentions, beliefs, and desires to Large Language Models (LLMs). Building on ecological psychology, we argue that this tendency is neither a mere illusion nor solely dependent on understanding the model's internal mechanics.

Instead, intentionality emerges from the interactive relation between the human user and the model's context-sensitive responsiveness. This account offers a middle path between strong internalism and deflationary illusionism, providing practical implications for AI safety, welfare, and regulation.
