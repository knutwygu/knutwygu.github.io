01 / About

An analog mind in a discrete world.

Hello, I'm Knut. I am an Electrical Engineering undergraduate at UC Irvine with a deep fascination for digital signal processing, circuit design, and the mathematics that govern physical systems.

My focus lies in extracting order from noise. I find a unique kind of satisfaction in understanding how continuous, chaotic signals can be tamed, sampled, and manipulated into discrete domains. Whether it's analyzing frequency spectrums, or designing hardware filters from scratch, I enjoy the entire pipeline of signal flow.

Currently, my work revolves around translating theoretical math into physical reality—from exploring edge detection through spatial and temporal high-pass filters, to building music-reactive hardware. I believe that good engineering should shape data with precision and style.

// Beyond the Lab

When I'm not in the lab, my interests still reflect a love for complex systems and sensory experiences. I'm a massive Formula 1 fan—endlessly fascinated by the real-time telemetry, sensor networks, and extreme engineering that define the sport. I also enjoy the tactile, modular satisfaction of building with LEGO, and you'll almost always find me listening to the cinematic soundscapes of Lana Del Rey while I work.

Looking ahead, I want to apply my foundation in DSP and hardware to solve real-world challenges. I’m especially drawn to industries focused on multimodal perception and intelligent sensing, such as audio and speech technologies, autonomous systems, and applied machine learning. My ultimate goal is to build systems that help machines perceive, process, and interact with the physical world more elegantly.
