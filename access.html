Fixing a broken headphone jack (Soldering)

THEORY: Circuit Continuity & Impedance.
A broken wire creates an "Open Circuit" (Infinite Impedance), meaning no current flows to the driver. The 3.5mm jack is a TRS connector: Tip (Left), Ring (Right), Sleeve (Ground).

REALITY: Don't throw it away. Strip the wire, match the colors (Red=Right, Blue/Green=Left, Copper=Ground). Burn off the enamel coating with solder (it acts as flux). Re-establish continuity. You just saved $20.

Why chargers get hot (Power Dissipation)

THEORY: Joule Heating (P = IÂ²R).
No voltage converter is 100% efficient. Your charger steps down 120V AC to 5V DC. The lost energy is dissipated as heat due to internal resistance in the transformer and rectifier diodes.

REALITY: It's normal for electronics to get warm. However, if it's too hot to touch, the internal resistance has spiked (likely a failing capacitor). Safety Tip: Never charge your phone under a pillow; you are blocking the heat dissipation path!

Logic gates inside your coffee machine

THEORY: Finite State Machine (FSM).
The machine operates on simple Boolean Logic. It uses an AND Gate structure:
IF (Water_Level == HIGH) AND (Button == PRESSED) THEN (Pump = ON).

REALITY: Next time it doesn't work, think like a debugger. Is the input sensor (water tank) failing? Or is the switch (button) bouncing? It's not magic; it's just a conditional statement waiting for True inputs.
