Why speakers hum (Ground Loops)

THEORY Ground Potential Difference (V = IR).
"Ground" is rarely 0V everywhere. When devices connect to different outlets, they form a giant conductive loop. This loop acts like an antenna, picking up 60Hz AC noise that gets amplified as that annoying "buzz."

REALITY It's not a ghost in the machine; it's physics. The best fix isn't expensive cables, but breaking the loop with a Ground Loop Isolator. Pro Tip: Before buying gear, try plugging your PC and speakers into the same power strip to minimize the loop area!

Why chargers get hot (Power Dissipation)

THEORY Joule Heating (P = I²R).
No voltage converter is 100% efficient. Your charger steps down 120V AC to 5V DC. The lost energy is dissipated as heat due to internal resistance in the transformer and rectifier diodes.

REALITY It's normal for electronics to get warm. However, if it's too hot to touch, the internal resistance has spiked (likely a failing capacitor). Safety Tip: Never charge your phone under a pillow; you are blocking the heat dissipation path!

Logic gates inside your coffee machine

THEORY Boolean Logic (Y = A · B).
Your machine is effectively a simple computer. It uses a hardware AND Gate structure for safety:
IF (Water_Level == 1) AND (Button == 1) THEN (Pump = ON). If either input is 0, the system stays idle.

REALITY Next time it fails, think like a debugger: which input is sending a "False" signal? Pro Tip: The water tank's magnetic float sensor often gets stuck due to scale buildup. A quick shake is usually all it needs to flip that sensor bit back to "1"!
